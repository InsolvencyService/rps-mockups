Stuff we have tested

Wednesday 19th March

  • Scenario 1
    • Did not claim benefits
    • Was refused Job Seekers Allowance because had not made enough NI contributions - they will have a letter detailing this.
    • Employed by Brentshire Utilities from 6th January 2014 and ongoing. Earned £350 gross and paid £350 per week during period up to 13th January 2014. Worked 37 hours per week.
  • Scenario 2
    • From 5th Nov – 13th Jan/ongoing earned extra money working for another company they were employed by before. They worked an extra 10 hours per week. And were paid £10 per hour. The amount this added up to was £1000. (10 hours, 10 weeks, £1000 extra over the period)
    • They had an increase in their housing benefit over full period of 10 weeks, it used to be £200 per two weeks and is now £300 per two weeks. So extra was £100x5 = £500.
    • Applied for JSA but was refused because they hadn’t paid enough NI contributions – they will have a letter detailing this.
  • Scenario 3
    • From 5th Nov – 13th Jan/ongoing they’ve been self employed. They invoiced for £1000 and spent £500.
    • Had additional employment from 23rd December 2013 to 5th January 2014. Working for Brentshire Utilities, earning gross £350 per week. Total of £700 for the two weeks. Worked 37 hours per week.
    • Did not claim for any benefits. Did not try to claim.
  • Scenario 4
    • Claimed Job seekers allowance twice over the notice period. They had a holiday in the middle.
    • Dates claimed JSA:
      • 8th Nov 2013– 16th Dec 2013: Claimed 5 weeks at £71.50 = £357.50 plus 3 days £21.42. Total claimed: £378.92
      • and
      • 2nd January 2014 – 13th Jan 2014/Ongoing: Claimed one full week £71.50 plus 4 days £40.85. Total claimed: £112.35
    • Had no period of employment
    • Had no period of self employment
